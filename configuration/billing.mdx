---
title: "Riftmap subscription plans, limits, and billing management"
description: "Understand Riftmap's free and paid plans, how plan limits are enforced, and how to upgrade, manage seats, export your data, or cancel your subscription."
---

Riftmap offers a free plan and paid plans (Team and Business) with higher repository, seat, and org connection limits. Limits are enforced at the API level — when you hit a limit, you receive a `402 Payment Required` response with an upgrade hint rather than a silent failure. This page covers what each plan includes and how to manage your subscription.

## Plan overview

| Plan | Repositories | Seats | Org connections |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | 15 | 1 | 1 |
| **Team** | 100 | 5 + add-ons | 1 |
| **Business** | 500 | 15 + add-ons | Unlimited |

<Note>
  If you are running a self-hosted Riftmap deployment without a Stripe key configured, billing is disabled and no plan limits are enforced. See [Configure a self-hosted Riftmap deployment](/configuration/environment).
</Note>

## How limits are enforced

Riftmap checks plan limits at the moment you take an action — not retroactively. Existing data is never automatically deleted when you downgrade.

| Action | Enforcement point |
|---|---|
| Connect an org | Blocked at the moment of connection if you are at your org connection limit |
| Invite a member | Blocked when the invitation is accepted if you are at your seat limit |
| Trigger a scan | Blocked at the moment of trigger if your total repository count exceeds the plan limit |

When a limit is reached, the API returns `402` with a human-readable message and an `upgrade_url`. The web app surfaces this as an in-line upgrade prompt rather than a generic error.

<Tip>
  If you are over a limit after a downgrade (for example, you have more connected orgs than your new plan allows), Riftmap shows a warning banner in **Settings → Billing** but does not delete your data. You can manually disconnect orgs or remove members to come back within limits.
</Tip>

## View your current plan and usage

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web app">
    Go to **Settings → Billing**. You will see your current plan, usage bars for repositories, seats, and org connections, and the next billing date.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="API">
    ```bash
    GET /api/v1/workspaces/{id}/billing
    ```

    Returns your plan, seat counts, `cancel_at` (if a cancellation is pending), and whether you are over any limits (`over_seat_limit`, `over_org_limit`, `over_repo_limit`). Requires owner role.

    For a detailed breakdown of usage vs. plan limits:

    ```bash
    GET /api/v1/workspaces/{id}/billing/usage
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Upgrade your plan

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web app">
    Go to **Settings → Billing → Upgrade Plan**. Select Team or Business and complete the Stripe Checkout flow. Your plan is activated as soon as payment succeeds.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="API">
    ```bash
    POST /api/v1/workspaces/{id}/billing/checkout
    Content-Type: application/json

    { "plan": "team" }
    ```

    Returns `{ "checkout_url": "..." }`. Redirect your user to `checkout_url` to complete payment. Requires owner role.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Manage seats

Seat add-ons let you increase your seat count beyond the plan default without switching to a higher plan.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add seats">
    In the app: go to **Settings → Billing → Seats → Add seats**. Via API: `POST /workspaces/{id}/billing/seats` with body `{ "additional_seats": N }`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove seats">
    In the app: **Settings → Billing → Seats → Remove seats**. Via API: `DELETE /workspaces/{id}/billing/seats` with body `{ "seats_to_remove": N }`. If removing seats would put your workspace over the new limit, you will be asked to select members to remove first.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cancel your subscription

Cancelling sets `cancel_at_period_end` on your Stripe subscription. Your workspace keeps full access until the end of the current billing period — there is no immediate termination.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Cancel in the app">
    Go to **Settings → Billing → Cancel subscription**. You can optionally provide a cancellation reason.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resume before the period ends">
    Changed your mind? Go to **Settings → Billing → Resume subscription** any time before the cancellation date. Via API: `POST /workspaces/{id}/billing/resume`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  After cancellation takes effect, your workspace reverts to the free plan limits (15 repos, 1 seat, 1 org connection). Your data is not deleted.
</Note>

## Export your workspace data (GDPR)

You can export all workspace data as a JSON file at any time. The export includes members, connected orgs (without access tokens), repositories, scans, dependency declarations, and graph edges.

```bash
GET /api/v1/workspaces/{id}/export
```

The response is a downloadable JSON attachment. This endpoint requires owner or admin role and is rate-limited to one export per workspace per hour.

<Warning>
  If you call this endpoint more than once within an hour, you will receive a `429 Too Many Requests` response. Wait until the hour window resets before requesting another export.
</Warning>
